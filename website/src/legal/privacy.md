# Privacy Policy

**Tasca**

**Effective date:** June 1, 2026
**Last updated:** June 1, 2026

## 1. Who we are and what this Policy covers

This Privacy Policy explains how ICEMINT, LLC ("**Company**," "**we**," "**us**") collects, uses, shares, and protects information in connection with the Tasca hosted service at tasca.dev and associated applications, APIs, and websites (collectively, the "**Service**"). It is incorporated into and governed by our Terms of Service.

Tasca is an AI agent workforce platform: you build a roster of named AI agents, give each a native identity inside the tools your team uses (such as Shortcut, GitHub, and Linear), and Tasca's routing engine assigns tasks to the right agent across your projects.

**Hosted Service; execution on your infrastructure.** This Policy describes the **hosted** Service that Company operates at tasca.dev (the roster control plane, routing engine, and APIs). Portions of agent execution may run on third-party platforms you connect (such as Shortcut, GitHub, and Linear) or on infrastructure you provide or authorize (for example, your own host or GPU machine over SSH, or local AI models you operate). For those third-party platforms and your own infrastructure, you are the operator/controller, their own terms govern, and this Policy does not extend to them. The hosted control plane is designed to operate without embedding third-party product-analytics telemetry within the application itself; see Section 6.

## 2. Information we collect

We collect the following categories in operating the hosted Service:

**Account and identity data.** Name, email address, hashed password, and authentication state. Where you sign in via a third-party identity provider (e.g., GitHub or Google) using OAuth, we receive the profile information that provider returns and authorization tokens, which are stored encrypted.

**Roster, agent, and work data.** Organizations, projects, your named agents and their capability profiles and identity bindings, tasks and work-orders synced from connected platforms, routing decisions, assignments, throughput and performance metrics, and activity/audit-timeline records generated as you and your team use the Service.

**Connected-credential and identity data.** Third-party credentials, keys, and tokens you choose to connect — including AI model-provider API keys (e.g., Anthropic and OpenAI keys), local-model endpoints, project-platform tokens (Shortcut, GitHub, Linear), source-control and identity-provider authorizations, and any SSH or host credentials you provide for remote execution. These are stored encrypted and used to provide the features you enable and to provision and operate the native agent identities you authorize.

**Code and execution data.** Repository content, worktrees, diffs, pull-request metadata, prompts, agent outputs, and execution logs processed to run agent tasks you initiate. Depending on your configuration, this processing may occur on Company-coordinated runners or on infrastructure you provide or authorize (such as your own host over SSH or your local models).

**Integration data.** Information exchanged with the platforms you connect (e.g., Shortcut, GitHub, and Linear app/agent installations, webhooks, assignment and review events).

**Support and communications.** Information you provide when you contact us.

**Website/technical data.** Basic technical information necessary to deliver the website and Service (e.g., IP address, device and browser information, and request logs), as described in Section 6.

## 3. How we use information

We use information to: provide, operate, secure, and improve the Service; authenticate users and protect accounts; route and execute agent tasks you initiate; integrate with services you connect; provide support; comply with law; and enforce our Terms. We process Customer Content only to provide the Service to you and as you direct, or as required by law.

## 4. How we share information

We share information only as follows: (a) with **service providers and infrastructure vendors** that host or support the Service, under contractual confidentiality and security obligations; (b) with **AI model providers you connect or direct us to use** (such as Anthropic and OpenAI) — for example, when the PM-assistant or a worker agent sends task content to a model provider's API under your configured key, that content is transmitted to and processed by that provider under its terms; (c) with the **project platforms, source-control, and identity providers you connect** (such as Shortcut, GitHub, and Linear), including to provision and operate the native agent identities you authorize; (d) in connection with a **merger, acquisition, or sale of assets**, subject to this Policy; and (e) where **required by law** or to protect rights, safety, and the security of the Service. **We do not sell your personal information.**

## 5. International data transfers

The Service is operated from, and information may be processed in, the United States and other countries where we or our service providers operate. If you access the Service from outside the United States, you understand that your information may be transferred to and processed in countries whose data-protection laws may differ from those of your country. Where applicable law requires a transfer mechanism, we will rely on a lawful mechanism. **Execution on your own infrastructure:** where you direct agent execution to run on infrastructure you control (for example, your own host over SSH or local AI models you operate), the associated code and execution data may remain on the infrastructure you control and may not be transmitted to Company. **International users:** see Section 11.2.

## 6. Tracking technologies and consent

The Tasca **application / control plane** does not embed third-party advertising, analytics, session-replay, or heat-mapping technologies and does not send third-party product-usage telemetry to outside analytics providers. We process only the technical request data necessary to operate and secure the Service (Section 2).

**Website and documentation analytics — Fathom Analytics.** Our **website and documentation properties** at tasca.dev use **Fathom Analytics** to understand aggregate traffic and usage. Fathom is a privacy-focused, **cookieless** web-analytics service: it does not use cookies, does not collect or store personally identifying information for cross-site tracking, and reports only aggregate, non-identifying statistics (such as total page views, referrers, and general country/region). We do not use advertising or attribution pixels, session recording or replay, heat-mapping, or email/CRM tracking pixels on our properties.

> **Publisher note (delete before publishing):** Confirm this description against Fathom's current documentation before publishing, and keep it current if you add any other tools. If your organization needs a data-processing agreement for Fathom, obtain and reference theirs. Re-audit your properties quarterly.

**Consent.** Because our website analytics are cookieless and do not collect personally identifying information for cross-site tracking, we do not display a tracking-cookie banner for them. To the extent any optional, non-essential data collection is introduced in the future, we will obtain consent where required by applicable law (see Section 11). You may further limit data collection using standard browser controls, Do Not Track, and Global Privacy Control signals.

**Cookies and essential storage.** We use only **strictly necessary** cookies and similar local storage — the kind required to operate the Service, such as keeping you signed in, maintaining your session, remembering your theme/interface preferences, and protecting against security threats. These are essential to provide the Service you request, are not used for advertising or cross-site tracking, and therefore do not require a consent banner. We do not set advertising, analytics, or third-party tracking cookies. You can block or delete cookies through your browser settings, but doing so may prevent you from signing in or using parts of the Service.

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
